The Problem

Airborne pathogens are the invisible threat in indoor agriculture. Botrytis spores, Powdery Mildew conidia, Aspergillus, and bacterial aerosols circulate through HVAC systems and open grow rooms — landing on crops, colonizing surfaces, and spreading contamination faster than any surface treatment can contain.

HEPA filtration and UV systems help but leave gaps. Filters capture particles but don't kill them. UV only treats what passes directly through the light path. Neither addresses pathogens already suspended in room air or settling on canopy surfaces.

How AIRRox Works

AIRRox introduces EPA-registered chlorine dioxide in controlled gas-phase concentrations into your facility's airspace. At managed levels, ClO₂ gas neutralizes airborne mold spores, bacteria, and viral particles — continuously, 24/7 — without harming crops, workers, or equipment.

Gas-Phase Treatment

ClO₂ gas reaches every corner of your grow room — including above canopy, behind equipment, and inside HVAC ductwork — areas that sprays and filters miss.

Spore Neutralization

Destroys Botrytis, Powdery Mildew, Aspergillus, and Fusarium spores while they're still airborne — before they land on your crop.

Continuous Protection

AIRRox runs 24/7 at crop-safe concentrations. No downtime, no cycling, no gaps in coverage — persistent protection around the clock.

Crop Safe

At managed PPB-level concentrations, ClO₂ gas does not harm plant tissue, trichomes, terpenes, or beneficial insects used in IPM programs.

Real-Time Monitoring

Integrated sensors track ClO₂ concentration levels in real time. Our team monitors remotely and adjusts dosing as environmental conditions change.

EPA Registered

All AIRRox products and application methods are EPA-registered for use in agricultural environments. Fully compliant, fully documented.
